# Sway Revenue Model

## Primary Revenue Streams

- Platform fee per paid request, boost, or tip.
- Performer-paid subscription for advanced queue tools, analytics, and reusable gig kits.
- Future Sway distribution fee per paid, pre-tax original-audio line item: 20% below $5 rounded down to the cent in the creator's favor, otherwise a flat $1; refunds reverse that fee.
- Future separately contracted merch fulfillment, ticketed show, and paid-stream services.
- Featured placement for venue discovery once real marketplace data exists.
- Venue or event operator plans for multi-performer reporting.

## Fee Rules

The app must only show fee language that matches the implemented payment processor flow. If the system captures immediately, copy must say so. If it authorizes first and captures later, the backend ledger and processor state must prove that behavior.

## Launch Constraint

Featured placement and venue discovery should remain limited until performer listings are backed by real accounts and active gigs. Public revenue claims should be avoided until payment and payout reporting is production-grade.

## Future Creator-Audio Revenue Lane

The owner-set Sway distribution fee is 20% of a paid audio line item priced below $5, rounded down to the cent in the creator's favor, or a flat $1 for an item priced at $5 or more. Separately stated tax is outside the fee base, and a refunded sale reverses the associated Sway fee. Processor fees, reserves, chargebacks, creator allocations, and statutory royalty obligations remain separate and must be disclosed.

Under the target model, Sway is the distributor, not the copyright owner. Artists, producers, writers, and other collaborators retain or allocate their master, composition, producer, and revenue rights through their own versioned agreements. Sway receives a limited distribution authorization and its disclosed service fee, not ownership or label points.

Audio sales, collaborator splits, distribution royalties, composition publishing income, and live-room tips/requests/boosts are separate ledgers and contracts. None may reuse another lane's payment records. A future implementation requires contract-versioned terms, idempotent orders and provider events, append-only royalty entries, reconciled statements, KYC/tax handling, transparent deductions, and exportable artist accounting before public fee claims.

Future merch fulfillment, ticketed shows, and paid streams are also separate products. Their costs and fees must be approved and displayed independently; they do not change the $1/20% distribution rule or silently stack onto an audio purchase.
